Deep features based convolutional neural network to detect and automatic classification of white blood cells

Abstract

White blood cells (WBCs) play an important role in the human body's immune system and it widely presented in the human body. Recently, the incidence of blood diseases related to WBC increases in the human body. In the research application, optimal WBC is a popular field for the diagnosis of blood disease, the optimal WBC count gives useful information. The Deep Features Based Convolutional Neural Network (DFCNN) is created in this research to detect the count of WBC and automated categorization using CNN from the picture database. With three phases, the proposed method working are feature extraction, feature selection, and classification phases. In the extraction phase, the Combined CNN structure is designed which is a combination of AlexNet, GoogLeNet, and ResNet-50 respectively. The combined CNN architecture is utilized to extract 3000 essential features from the image database. In the feature selection phase, the hybrid Mayfly Algorithm with Particle Swarm Optimization (HMA-PSO) is designed for selecting the essential feature from the feature sets. In the HMA-PSO algorithm, with the help of PSO algorithm, the velocity updating of the mayfly is achieved. The proposed classifier named Recurrent Neural Network-Long Short-Term Memory (RNN-LSTM), collects the selected features. This classifier is utilized to classify the WBC types such as Neutrophils, Eosinophils, Monocytes, and Lymphocytes respectively. The proposed method is implemented in the platform of MATLAB and performances are evaluated by statistical measurements such as precision, recall, specificity, accuracy, and F_Measure. The results of the proposed method are compared with the other existing methods such as MA-RNN and PSO-RNN respectively.
